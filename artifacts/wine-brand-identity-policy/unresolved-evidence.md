# Artwork-only limits — what cannot be established from the label

For each situation, the §4.33(a) trigger ("if not sold under a brand name") turns
on marketing/permit facts a label image does not carry.

## The brand-absent fallback question (controls)

`approved-wine-022` and `approved-wine-082` are annotated brand-**absent**, yet
each forbids a responsible-person-style name (`Marble Creek Acres`; `3 Steves
Winery`). Under §4.33(a), a wine with **no** brand name has its responsible
person *deemed* the brand — so "brand-absent" and "a responsible person exists"
may be in tension **if** these are complete labels. If they are partial/back
panels (brand on another panel), there is no tension. **The artwork shown does
not settle which.** This is flagged, not resolved, and it is out of scope for the
primary 13.

## What artwork *can* establish (so uncertainty is not overused)

- A prominent, stylized front-label identity mark is strong evidence of an
  explicit marketed brand (e.g. `M Cellars`, `Duck Walk Vineyards`, `Dry Cellar`).
- A §4.35 lead-in (*produced/bottled/imported by* + name) reliably marks a name as
  a responsible-person statement.
- Varietal, class/type, and appellation are identifiable as designations from the
  wording alone (§4.34).

Uncertainty should be reserved for genuine brand-role ambiguity, not applied to
every company name.
